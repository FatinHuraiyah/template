Role and function of Research in Chemical Engineering

According to Dr. Chong Mee Yoke the role of research in Chemical Engineering is to find solutions to problems by discovering new things and proving theories through experiments. When asked "What is the role of research in Chemical Engineering?" to Dr Chong Mee Yoke, this is what she answered:

"Research is the main purpose of a chemical engineer especially if you work in R&D. You apply what you know to real life situation, you test your theoretical knowledge to life situation and see if it works and if it doesn't you need to figure out why and develop a solution. With this solution and new things that you learn, you can invent new technology that will make the world a better place."

The role of research in Chemical Engineering is just like other scientific research that is to explain the world around us and to understand how the universe operates. The function of research in Chemical Engineering is to seek answers to different questions on how and why something exists or happens. In broader term, the purpose of research in Chemical Engineering is to understand the underlying principles of existence. With more comprehensive knowledge and understanding on how the world works, chemical engineers are able to create new technologies and substances. The research in Chemical Engineering involves primarily in testing out the theories made by pure sciences by applying it real situations addressing more than just abstract principles. Chemical engineers test the theories to real situations using scientific tools and methods to test it vigorously. This is an ongoing process on refining hypotheses, until a certain hypothesis is accepted which is known as the scientific truth.

The researches done within Chemical Engineering are divided into nine research areas. This is to ensure a more systematic data collection and presentation which will ease one researcher to access another researcher's research and also to form collaborations. The nine areas are Thermodynamics and Molecular Computations, Catalysis and Reaction Engineering, Systems Design and Engineering, Transport Processes, Biological Engineering, Materials, Polymers, Surfaces and Structures, and lastly, Energy and Environmental Engineering. All 9 research areas have the ultimate objective that is to test theories made by pure science to real situations but each one differs in specific materials that they are testing. For example, System Design and Engineering focuses on research that involves designing a new technology that will stimulate the behavior of chemical processes and discovering the optimum operating conditions for chemical processes which in the end results in improvement in economy, reliability and safety. On the other hand, Energy and Environmental Engineering focuses on research that explores new sources of energy or refining the current process for harvesting energy and at the same time address the world's energy and its environmental impact. Both research areas involve inventing new technologies but the difference is the purpose the technologies are invented for. In conclusion, scientific research is the backbone of Chemical Engineering as an academic discipline. The scientific research must be done professionally, adhering to specific format and supported with verified evidences obtained from experiments.
