Background Analysis

"Food, Inc". will make you view foods in way you have never imagined before. The next time you buy a juicy T-bone or that yummy-looking chicken breast, you cannot help but to imagine a cow spending his days in ankle-height manure eating nothing but corns or a chicken that is trapped helplessly in a dark and small cage. You will realize what you consume everyday has much more secrets within it than you thought.

"Food, Inc". is a groundbreaking-American documentary film directed by Robert Kenner who is an Emmy Award-winning filmmaker and narrated by Michael Pollan and Eric Schlosser. The documentary film was released in 2008 and has won a fourth place as best documentary at the 35th Seattle International Film Festival. "Food, Inc." has tracked down the food source in America which is the corporate farming and made many surprising discoveries that prove the declining quality of food source at the same time is harmful to the environment and abusive to both animals and employees. Does it ever cross you mind that maybe the food industries do not want us to know where our food comes from? Food, Inc. reveals the answer to that question through the 94-minutes documentary, "Food, Inc.".

The food agriculture is closely related to the expanding fast food culture in the United States. McDonald's is the largest purchaser of ground beef, pork, chicken, potatoes, cabbages, tomatoes and even apples in the United States. McDonald's introduce a revolutionary idea on how to run a restaurant. The McDonald's Brothers want to cut the costs and simplify the process of food production and they achieve this by creating a factory system at the back of the restaurant kitchen. Fast food industries like McDonald's want their food to have consistent taste for every franchise. Since they cover a great area in the United States; they play a great influence on the raw food system in the United States.

The food industry has changed more in the last 50 years than in the previous ten thousands. The fast food industry plays a major role in the food industry that the system of the food industry revolves around the necessities of the fast food industry. The ultimate aim of the current food industry is to produce a lot of food in small amount of land at a very affordable price and in small period of time. The quality of the food is sacrificed for profits of the companies.

What we see on the shelf in groceries store are mostly delusional. Average grocery store has 47, 000 products and it made the consumer think like they have variety of choice but the ugly truth is they are only a few major companies and a few major crops are involved. What is inside the products are practically the same, they only differ in the corn rearrangement. They are too many corns around United States that the food scientist had to figure out ways to make use of the corns. As a result, 90% of the processed food contains either a corn or soybean ingredient or even both; so, the consumers are eating less variety then they thought. Cows that naturally eat grass are trained to eat corn instead because farmers figured that corns are so much cheaper than grass. Not only cows, chickens and even fishes are also fed with corns.

Since corn, wheat and soybeans are heavily subsidized by the United States government, many unhealthy snacks and fast foods are cheaper than healthy and fresh produces. For example, a double-cheeseburger from McDonald's is 99 cents and that is even cheaper than the head of a broccoli. This evidently leads to people opting for a less healthy choice of food and thus leading to increasing of many weight-related diseases. Type 2 diabetes which only affect adult in the old times are now starting to affect children in epidemic proportions.

Apart from the public ignorance they are few other factors that result in deteriorating food industry. The failure to enforce Genetically Modified Organism (GMO) label on food, the Veggie Libel Laws, Cheeseburger bills make it harder for the public to make a change in the food industry as the food industry has a different protections than other industries.
