Description of the Issue

This proposal will focus on the effect that the fast food companies have on the food industry in the United States, how it affects the public's health and what can we do to overcome the problems? Today, United States is known as the fast food nation. At least one out of four American adults eats fast food every day. It is common knowledge to everyone that fast food is unhealthy but why is there majority of people still consuming it? The American lifestyle is built around speed and efficiency. Everything must be done in a very efficient way but in a short amount of time and many technologies; like lap tops, smart phones and cars, are built to fulfill this necessity. All these greatly increase the efficiency; but sadly somewhere along this advancement, the traditional home-cooked meal has given its way to fast food.

A student might be late for classes and has no time for breakfast, has homework that due that morning and exams during the afternoon and classes in the evening and most probably only will be back home by night. With her packed schedule, the most convenient option she has is the vending machine or take-out food. By simply looking at this, we can understand why it is easy for fast food to replace home-cooked meal. "Fast food is very convenient!" is what most of people including myself would think. But is fast food really worth it? After watching the "Food, Inc.", I doubt that fast food is really worth it. For once, I too thought that as long as it is filling then the food is enough but essentially food is more than something consumed just to fill you up, it affects your health and your life significantly.

The fast food industries are playing such a prominent role in our daily life. Since they are the major buyer of food produces, the modern agriculture are shaped in the way to fulfill the demands of the fast food industries. Tomatoes are ripened using ethylene gas, chickens are genetically modified to have larger breast and most animals are trained to eat corns because corn is the cheapest grain available. These cause problem to us; for example, cow that consume corn is prone to has E. Coli, which is a serious disease that can cause death to human. The modern agriculture is all about producing something faster, fatter, bigger and cheaper. All they want is to maximize their profits without thinking about the quality of the produces they produce and this will affect us, the consumer, greatly. So, what are we supposed to do now?
