Proposed Solutions and Anticipated Results or Effects

Proposed Solutions

Every big change always starts from small changes. Consumers are able to change the food industry if they stand together and fight for their rights. This is because the consumers are the one that has the money power, who is buying the groceries sold at the market. A good example of this is, now, due to the demand of the a big number of consumer, the market has now offer organic options for groceries items like eggs, milks, and meat produces. Wal-Mart, the biggest retailer has offer organic option for milk due to the pressure from the consumer. The next important thing that we can do as consumers is to change the policy level so that the price of healthier foods will be lower than those of unhealthy ones. Instead of subsiding heavily on grains, the government can decrease the subsidies on the grains and start or increase the subsidies on fruits and vegetables. Next time people are finding something to snack on they would not think twice on opting for a healthier choice as it is cheaper than the unhealthier ones.

Simple changes such as filing your kitchen cabinets and refrigerator with in seasonal, and organic food options, reading labels when buying groceries and start cooking a proper home meal for your own family will make big changes in your life. Parents should show good eating habits to their children. They can always train their children to love healthy food by simple acts like replacing the snack stocks with fruit stocks and making eating at home a habit. The world can be changed with every little bite!

Anticipated Results or Effects

If this fast food culture is not handled with care it might cause a severe domino effect not only on the Americans but also the world. Due to the economic vitality, the fast food nation in the United States is exported to the rest of the world. There are 28, 000 McDonald's franchises worldwide, and four out of five newly opened branched of McDonald's are from outside of the United States. Fast foods are high in unhealthy fats, artificial flavorings, food colorings, excess carbohydrates and sugars; and these will cause cardiovascular disease, liver disease, cancer or type-2 diabetes. It is already very worrying when adults are having these diseases but it more alarming when the children are having these diseases too. Currently, one out of three children is either overweight or obese. If this goes on, we will be facing a serious health problem in both adult and children. Immediate steps must be taken to reverse the frightening rise in obesity.
